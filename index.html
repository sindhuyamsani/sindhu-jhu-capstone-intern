The Framework

Three phases — one goal

Phase 01

Acquire

Find companies who are the right fit — not just willing, but set up for a 7–8 week engagement to succeed.

Phase 02

Form

Scope the problem in a way that works for both the host and the student team.

Phase 03

Coordinate

Set clear expectations early and keep communication running smoothly once it starts.

Outreach Strategy

What I'd do — and what I'd do differently

📄
Key Idea

Build a case study from a past capstone

A one-pager showing a real past project — what the problem was, what the team delivered, what the company did with it. Makes the pitch concrete. Easy to forward internally to get buy-in.

🔁

Start with warm outreach

Past hosts already understand the program. They say yes faster.

🔗

Mine the Hopkins network

Alumni, faculty connections, JHU career office — warm intros convert better than cold emails.

⚡

Lower the entry barrier

Offer a 15-min no-commitment call. Add a simple FAQ so companies know exactly what they're signing up for before getting on a call.

💼

LinkedIn + alumni outreach

Post about the program and reach out directly to alumni at companies that would be good hosts.

Partner Vetting

How I'd decide if a company is a good fit

Criteria Priority What I look for
Host availability
Must-have
Can they show up weekly for 7–8 weeks? If not, the project fails.
Problem scope
Must-have
Right-sized — not too broad, not too narrow. Shapeable in a scoping conversation.
Technicality fit
Must-have
Host understands this is strategy/process — not an engineering project.
Decision-making access
Important
Is our contact senior enough to actually act on recommendations?
Student learning potential
Important
Will students walk away with a story worth telling in their next interview?
Hopkins connection
Nice to have
Alumni, faculty link, or past collaboration — makes coordination easier.
